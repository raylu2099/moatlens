# The Five Types of Economic Moat

> "The key to investing is not assessing how much an industry is going to
> affect society, or how much it will grow, but rather determining the
> competitive advantage of any given company and, above all, the
> durability of that advantage."
>
> — **Warren Buffett**

## Traditional five categories

### 1. Brand (pricing power)

The ability to charge more than competitors for a functionally similar
product. Measured by:
- Gross margin premium vs industry
- Customer willingness to pay at price hikes
- Market share stability during downturns

**Classic examples**: Coca-Cola, Apple, LVMH, Nike, Moutai
**Warning**: Brands can erode (Sears, Gap, Avon). Look for 50+ years of
brand strength as the high bar.

### 2. Network Effects

Each new user makes the product more valuable for existing users. Often
winner-takes-most or winner-takes-all market dynamics.

**Types**:
- Two-sided (Uber drivers ↔ riders)
- Data (Google searches improve Google)
- Social (Meta: friends attract friends)

**Classic examples**: Visa/Mastercard, Meta, Google, Airbnb
**Caution**: Network effects can be disrupted by bundle plays (WeChat replacing
multiple apps) or by generational shifts (Facebook → Instagram → TikTok).

### 3. Switching Costs

The financial, time, or psychological cost for a customer to change
providers.

**Types**:
- Data lock-in (Microsoft Office files, Salesforce CRM data)
- Integration (ServiceNow deployments, SAP)
- Certification (AWS-certified engineers)
- Habit (Bloomberg Terminal muscle memory)

**Classic examples**: Microsoft, Oracle, SAP, Autodesk, Intuit (QuickBooks)
**Strength**: 15-40% annual price increases with <5% churn = world-class.

### 4. Scale / Cost Advantage

Large-scale operations create cost per unit lower than competitors. In some
industries this creates near-permanent dominance.

**Types**:
- Fixed cost leverage (Amazon AWS, Costco)
- Bargaining power (Walmart vs suppliers)
- Network density (FedEx, UPS)
- Geographic density (Cintas, USW)

**Classic examples**: Amazon Retail, Costco, UPS, Walmart, TSMC
**Test**: Can a competitor match the cost structure without years of investment?

### 5. Intangible Assets

Patents, regulatory approvals, licenses, government-granted monopolies.

**Types**:
- Patents (pharma, biotech)
- FDA / regulatory approvals (drug trials, medical devices)
- Franchises (railroads, cable companies historically)
- Data moats (credit bureaus, ratings agencies)

**Classic examples**: Moody's, S&P Global, pharma giants, Visa (regulatory
payment rails)

## Tech-era additions

Buffett's five categories were codified pre-2000. The software/platform
era creates additional moat types Munger later acknowledged:

### 6. Data Flywheel

More users → more data → better product → more users.

**Examples**: Google Search, Netflix recommendations, Tesla Autopilot,
TikTok For You Page.

### 7. Ecosystem Lock-In

Customers buy into an entire platform that's hard to extract from.

**Examples**: Apple (iMessage + iPhone + Mac + AirPods + Watch), Microsoft
365 + Azure + Teams, AWS (primary cloud + tools).

### 8. API / Developer Lock-In

Third-party developers build on your platform, creating a two-sided
network with developers as the "moat".

**Examples**: Stripe, Twilio, Salesforce AppExchange, iOS App Store.

## How to test moat durability

Ask these questions each year:
1. Has the company raised prices without losing market share?
2. Has share been stable or grown over 5+ years?
3. Have new entrants failed to gain meaningful traction?
4. Has the company been able to enter adjacent markets profitably?
5. In downturns, did revenue hold up better than industry average?

If 3+ yes, the moat is real. If 0-1 yes, it's probably a fashion or
temporary edge.

## The AI era question

For every tech audit Moatlens runs, Stage 3 asks explicitly: **is this
company's moat strengthening or weakening in the AI era?**

Some specific AI-era questions:
- Does AI commoditize your product? (translation, basic writing, simple
  coding) → moat weakening
- Does AI enhance your data flywheel? (recommendation, personalization)
  → moat strengthening
- Do you have proprietary data that AI needs? (medical records, financial
  data, industrial sensors) → moat strengthening
- Are you easily replaced by an API call? (customer support, content
  generation) → moat weakening

Categorize carefully.
